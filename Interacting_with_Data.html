Voter Trends and Turnouts in Virginia

Education and Income Level in Relation to Voting Frequency

In this graph, it can be seen that as voter frequency increases, the level of education and level of income increases.

"Sporadic" is the most common category of voting frequency whilst "Rarely/Never" is the least common. Overall, a higher education level is associated with a higher income level for voters.

Amount of Voter Turnout and Absentee Voting in VA Counties

This graph shows the change of absentee voting and voter turnout every two years from 2014 to 2022 in Virginia counties and cities.

2016 and 2020 are seen to have some of the greatest percent voter turnout across all regions, which is assumed to be because those years being presidential and congressional election years. Additionally, 2020 is seen to have the greatest % absent voters across all regions, which is assumed to be because it occured during the COVID-19 Pandemic, causing a surge in absentee voter numbers.

2022 Percent Voter Turnout in VA Counties

This graph differenties counties/cities in Virginia from one another based on percent voter turnout in 2022. It is seen through the use of a color scale that a majority of counties and cities had a voter turnout of around 50%.

Counties and cities with a higher percent voter turnout (in the 55-67% range) are located in more northern/eastern areas. This is represented with the darker purple colors in those areas. Those with a lower percent voter turnout (in the 33-46% range) are located in more southern/western areas.
